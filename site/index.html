How it works

Driver-first, not screen capture

The operating system genuinely composites everything at a higher resolution. No per-window capture, no overlay latency — the whole screen benefits at once.

01 · OS

Everything renders high

Desktop, browser, apps and games composite at a virtual resolution above what your monitor reports.

2880×1620 / 3840×2160
→
02 · GPU

Downsample in hardware

The driver's high-quality filter (13-tap Gaussian, or an AI DLDSR kernel) shrinks it to fit the panel.

DSR · DLDSR · VSR
→
03 · PANEL

Native signal, more detail

Your monitor still receives its native 1080p — but every edge and glyph carries supersampled clarity.

Architecture

Three planes in concert

A Rust core drives the hardware; a designed control center makes it effortless. The name is the metaphor — native at the center, resolutions orbiting like harmonics.

Tuner

Control plane · Rust

Talks to NVAPI, switches display modes, and compensates DPI so text stays the same physical size — just crisper. Every switch is guarded by a crash-safe revert timer.

Conductor

Automation · Rust

Declarative rules watch the foreground app and power source. Open Photoshop → DLDSR engages. Unplug the charger → back to native. You stop thinking about it.

Chamber

Control center · Tauri + Svelte

A tray-resident window with a harmonic-ring selector and spring-physics motion. A guided onboarding detects your GPU and walks you through the first switch safely.

Requirements

What you need

🪟

Windows 11

Windows 10 21H2+ supported on a best-effort basis.

🎛️

NVIDIA GPU with DSR

GTX 900-series or newer. DLDSR needs RTX (20-series+). AMD & Intel coming.

🖥️

Headroom to spare

Rendering at 4× the pixels costs GPU. Best on a card faster than your panel demands.

🆓
